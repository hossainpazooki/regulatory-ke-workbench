DLT ACT 2021 (SWITZERLAND)
Federal Act on the Adaptation of Federal Law to Developments in Distributed Ledger Technology
Key Provisions for Crypto Asset Regulation

FINMA TOKEN CLASSIFICATION (ICO Guidelines February 2018)

Three-tier classification based on economic function:

1. PAYMENT TOKENS
   - Definition: Tokens intended as means of payment for goods/services or value transfer
   - Examples: Bitcoin, Ether (as currency)
   - Regulatory treatment: Not securities; AML requirements apply
   - Key characteristic: No claims against issuer or third parties

2. UTILITY TOKENS
   - Definition: Tokens providing digital access to application or service via DLT infrastructure
   - Regulatory treatment: Minimal regulation if application already operational
   - Pre-functional tokens: May have investment element requiring securities treatment
   - Key characteristic: Access rights, not financial claims

3. ASSET TOKENS
   - Definition: Tokens representing assets (debt, equity, claims on real assets)
   - Examples: Tokenized shares, bonds, derivatives, real estate tokens
   - Regulatory treatment: Securities law applies (FMIA, FinSA)
   - Key characteristic: Economic function analogous to traditional securities

4. HYBRID TOKENS
   - Tokens combining functions of multiple categories
   - Must meet requirements of all applicable categories
   - Classification may change over time as token use evolves

FINANCIAL MARKET INFRASTRUCTURE ACT (FMIA) - SECURITIES DEFINITION

Article 2(b) - Securities:
Securities are certificated or uncertificated securities, derivatives, intermediated securities
or DLT Rights, which are:
(a) standardised and suitable for mass trading, meaning:
    - Offered publicly in same structure and denomination, OR
    - Placed with 20 or more clients under identical conditions

DLT ACT AMENDMENTS - KEY PROVISIONS

1. LEDGER-BASED SECURITIES (DLT Rights)
   - Legal recognition of securities issued on blockchain
   - Digital alternative to certificated securities
   - Ownership transfers recorded on distributed ledger have legal effect

2. DLT TRADING FACILITY LICENSE (FMIA Articles 73a-73f)
   - New FINMA license category for DLT trading venues
   - Multilateral trading of DLT securities (ledger-based securities)
   - May allow direct retail participation (unlike traditional exchanges)
   - Lower requirements than full stock exchange for small facilities
   - First license granted 2023 to BX Swiss subsidiary

3. CUSTODY SEGREGATION IN BANKRUPTCY (Banking Act Article 16)
   - Crypto assets individually allocated to customers can be segregated
   - Customers entitled to return of specifically allocated assets
   - Collective custody: proportional sharing if shortfall
   - Custodian must maintain records proving individual allocation

ANTI-MONEY LAUNDERING ACT (AMLA) - CRYPTO REQUIREMENTS

Applicability:
- Payment token services (exchange, transfer, custody, dealing)
- Must be FINMA-licensed or member of FINMA-recognized SRO

Key obligations:
1. Customer identification and verification (KYC)
2. Enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers/jurisdictions
3. Transaction monitoring and suspicious activity reporting
4. Record retention requirements

TRAVEL RULE (FINMA Implementation):
- Threshold: CHF 1,000 (stricter than FATF EUR/USD 1,000)
- Zero threshold for unverified or suspicious transfers
- Originator and beneficiary information must be collected and transmitted

STABLECOIN CLASSIFICATION (FINMA Supplement September 2019)

Stablecoins are NOT a separate category - classified by underlying rights:

1. FIAT-BACKED WITH REDEMPTION RIGHT
   - Classification: Deposit under Banking Act
   - Regulatory requirement: Banking license (or fintech license exception)
   - Rationale: Redemption claim = deposit claim

2. FIAT-BACKED WITHOUT REDEMPTION CLAIM
   - Classification: Payment token
   - Regulatory requirement: AML compliance only

3. ASSET-BACKED (Commodities, Securities, Baskets)
   - Classification: Asset token (security)
   - Regulatory requirement: Securities regulations
   - Additional: May trigger collective investment scheme rules

4. ALGORITHMIC (No backing)
   - Classification: Payment token
   - Regulatory requirement: AML compliance
   - Additional: Risk disclosure required

PROSPECTUS REQUIREMENTS (FinSA)

Public offerings of asset tokens require prospectus unless exemption applies:
- Qualified investors only
- Offering below threshold
- Limited number of offerees (<500)
- High minimum denomination (>100,000 CHF)

Prospectus must be approved by reviewing body before public offering.
