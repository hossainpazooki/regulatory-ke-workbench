PAYMENT SERVICES ACT 2019 (SINGAPORE)
Key Provisions for Digital Payment Token (DPT) Services

PART 2 - LICENSING OF PAYMENT SERVICE PROVIDERS

Section 6 - Licensing
(1) A person must not carry on a business of providing any payment service in Singapore
    unless the person is a licensee authorised to provide that payment service.
(2) A person who contravenes subsection (1) shall be guilty of an offence.

Section 7 - Types of Licenses
(1) Standard Payment Institution (SPI) license:
    - For providers with annual payment transactions up to S$3 million
    - For e-money issuers with outstanding e-money up to S$5 million
(2) Major Payment Institution (MPI) license:
    - For providers exceeding SPI thresholds
    - Minimum base capital requirement: S$250,000

DEFINITION OF DPT SERVICE (Section 2)

"digital payment token" means any digital representation of value that:
(a) is expressed as a unit;
(b) is not denominated in any currency;
(c) is, or is intended to be, a medium of exchange accepted by the public;
(d) can be transferred, stored or traded electronically; and
(e) satisfies such other characteristics as MAS may prescribe.

"digital payment token service" means any of the following services:
(a) dealing in digital payment tokens (DPT dealing);
(b) facilitating the exchange of digital payment tokens (DPT exchange);
(c) digital payment token transfer service (DPT transfer);
(d) digital payment token custodial service (DPT custody).

PART 3 - CONDUCT OF BUSINESS

Section 23 - Safeguarding of money received
DPT service providers must:
- Segregate customer digital payment tokens from operational assets
- Hold customer assets on statutory trust
- Maintain separate blockchain addresses for customer and firm assets
- Store at least 90% of customer assets in cold storage (MAS Guidelines)
- Conduct daily reconciliation of customer holdings

MAS NOTICE PSN02 - AML/CFT REQUIREMENTS

Key obligations for DPT service providers:
1. Appoint AML/CFT compliance officer at management level
2. Implement risk-based customer due diligence (CDD)
3. Conduct ongoing transaction monitoring
4. File Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with STRO
5. Retain CDD and transaction records for minimum 5 years
6. Implement Travel Rule for transfers >= CHF equivalent threshold

MAS STABLECOIN FRAMEWORK (August 2023)

Requirements for MAS-regulated single-currency stablecoins:
1. Issuer must be MAS-licensed entity
2. 100% reserve backing in cash, bank deposits, or government securities
3. Reserves segregated with accredited custodians
4. Annual audit with monthly attestations
5. Minimum base capital S$1,000,000 or 50% annual operating expenses
6. Redemption at par within 5 business days
7. Single-currency pegged to SGD or G10 currencies only

RETAIL CUSTOMER RESTRICTIONS (MAS Guidelines October 2022)

Prohibited activities for retail customers:
- DPT lending and borrowing services
- Staking services
- Margin or leveraged trading
- Public advertising (transport, public spaces)
- Trivializing cryptocurrency risks

FSMA 2022 - OFFSHORE SERVICES (Effective 30 June 2025)

Digital Token Service Provider (DTSP) licensing:
- Required for Singapore entities serving only offshore customers
- Applies to DPT and capital market tokens
- Does not apply to pure utility/governance tokens
- MAS applies high bar due to elevated ML risks
